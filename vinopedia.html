  • Cabernet Sauvignon

    Known for its bold and rich flavors with notes of black currant, tobacco, and sometimes green bell pepper.

  • Merlot

    Generally softer and more approachable than Cabernet Sauvignon, with flavors of plum, cherry, and chocolate.

  • Pinot Noir

    Often lighter in body with delicate flavors of red fruits like raspberry and cherry, along with earthy and floral notes.

  • Syrah

    Can vary in style, but often features flavors of dark fruits, black pepper, and sometimes smoky or spicy elements.

  • Zinfandel

    Known for its ripe, jammy flavors of blackberry and raspberry, often with a hint of spiciness.

  • Malbec

    Offers bold flavors of dark fruits like plum and blackberry, along with hints of cocoa and spices.

  • Sangiovese

    The main grape of Chianti, known for its bright red fruit flavors and high acidity.

  • Nebbiolo

    Primarily used in Barolo and Barbaresco wines from Italy, it has complex flavors of red fruit, tar, roses, and herbs.

  • Grenache

    Often found in blends, it can exhibit flavors of red fruit, spice, and sometimes a hint of earthiness.
